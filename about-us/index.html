Our History

In November 2011, Callisto CEO Jess Ladd founded Sexual Health Innovations (SHI) while she was a graduate student at Johns Hopkins University. SHI was founded with the goal of creating technology to advance sexual health and wellbeing in the United States.

In 2014, SHI announced an initiative called Callisto at The White House Data Jam on Campus Sexual Assault.

A year-long pilot of Callisto launched in August 2015 at the University of San Francisco and Pomona College. Callisto’s successful pilot, growing demand for the system both within and outside of the college setting, and a growing national dialogue on campus sexual violence led SHI to decide in the summer of 2016 to reorganize and rebrand as Callisto.

Today, Callisto is available at a growing number of colleges nationwide.

Front-End Engineer Contractor

About Callisto:

Callisto is a nonprofit dedicated to creating technology to combat sexual assault, empower survivors, and advance justice. We build an online sexual assault documentation and reporting system, and we partner with college campuses to provide school-customized Callisto websites for their students. We're launched on 8 campuses (the latest is Stanford) and will be in 12-15 in the fall. The most unique aspect of Callisto is our matching system that detects repeat offenders - allowing victims of the same perpetrator to "match" and report simultaneously. Here's a TED talk about how it works.

About the Tech:

Callisto’s current front-end stack is SCSS compiled with python, bootstrap, and a few small JS libraries. The front-end is being completely redone so you’ll have your choice of asset pipeline, compilers, front-end framework, etc. The only requirements are that your end product should be in SASS or SCSS and maintain our JS libraries functionality.
